Step 4: Enhance Windows Performance with Hellzerg's Optimizer

Warning: This tool hasn't been updated in over half a year. Use at your own risk:

Download Optimizer from Github (opens in a new tab)

Keep in mind: For certain settings you enable or disable, a restart may be required to apply the changes, which is found in the top right corner of the interface.

General Optimizer Settings:

These control system-wide optimizations like privacy, customization, and performance.

Windows-Specific Optimizer Settings:

These tailor optimizations to Windows 10/11 features, including privacy and security.

Enable or disable features according to your preferences.

  1. Explore the 'General' and 'Windows (Version)' settings for a comprehensive overview.
  2. Click on each option's text for a detailed description before applying changes.
  3. Personalize your experience by adjusting these settings. We've provided recommendations, but feel free to customize based on your needs.
  • 'General':
  • Print Service
  • Recommended to ensure printing functionality remains intact; do not disable.
  • Fax Service
  • Recommended if you need to send and receive faxes.
  • Superfetch
  • Recommended to have SysMain (Superfetch) enabled for HDD users; otherwise disable it. Limited RAM may slow down your HDD system.
  • SMBv2 Protocol
  • Recommended if using legacy printers. SMBv3 is more secure, though.
  • 'Windows (Version)':
  • Sensor Services
  • Recommended if your device has a touch function, such as a few laptops.
  • Virtualization Based Security
  • Recommended to turn off Virtualization-Based Security if you don't use both core isolation and virtualization, and do not care about HVCI.

Uncheck the option and click on 'Apply':

Step 6: Fine-Tune CPU Power Management with ParkControl

Download ParkControl from Bitsum (opens in a new tab)

For Laptops:

Disable both "Plugged In (AC)" and "On Battery (DC)". Turn off all park settings for optimal performance.

For Desktops:

Disable all park settings to maximize performance.

Key point: Enable 'Show power plan change notifications' and 'Show park settings in Windows Power Options' for easy access and monitoring.

Step 10: Advanced Optimization (BACKUP FIRST)

Backup Your Registry

Press the Windows key and type "reg edit" (short for Registry Editor).

Note: You need to restart your PC in order for it to take effect.

Optimize with Registry Editor (reg edit)

Navigate to:

HKEY_LOCAL_MACHINE\SYSTEM\CurrentControlSet\Control\PriorityControl

Enter one of the following values:

  • Gaming:
  • Hex: 14, 15, 16, 18 | Dec: 20, 21, 22, 24
  • Other:
  • Hex: 24, 25, 26, 2a, or 2 (default) | Dec: 36, 37, 38, 42, or 2

For further Registry Editor optimization, refer to: How to Change Split Threshold for svchost.exe in Windows 10 (opens in a new tab).
